Turtle Categories in Sri Lanka

Olive Ridley

Olive Ridley Turtles (Lepidochelys olivacea): Olive Ridley turtles are the most abundant species of sea turtles found in Sri Lanka. They are known for their olive-colored carapace and are primarily found in coastal waters and estuaries.

Hawksbill

Hawksbill Turtles (Eretmochelys imbricata): Hawksbill turtles are known for their unique overlapping scutes on their carapace, which gives them a serrated appearance. They have a pointed beak and are found in coral reef habitats. Sri Lanka's coral reefs provide important nesting grounds for Hawksbill turtles.

Loggerhead

Loggerhead Turtles (Caretta caretta): Loggerhead turtles are another species of sea turtles that can be found in Sri Lanka. They have a distinctive large head and strong jaws. Loggerhead turtles prefer warmer waters and are known to migrate long distances.

Leatherback

Leatherback turtles are the largest species of sea turtles and can grow up to 6 feet in length. They have a unique leathery shell instead of a hard one, which distinguishes them from other turtles. Leatherback turtles are rare in Sri Lanka but occasionally visit the island's waters.

Green Turtle

Green turtles are another common species found in Sri Lanka. They have a distinctive green-colored fat layer under their shells, which gives them their name. Green turtles are known for their herbivorous diet and can often be found feeding on seagrass beds.

Turtle Category Nesting sites Image
Green Turtle These turtles have been observed nesting in Sri Lanka's Akurella, Mawella, Kosgoda, Rekawa, Ambalantota, Yala, and Bundala.
Hawksbill Turtle Kataduwa, Bentota, Udappuwa, Kosgoda, Karaduwa, Talaimannar, Jaffna, Galkissa, Talawila, Palatupana, and Amadhauwa are among the nesting places in Sri Lanka.
Loggerhead Turtle They can be found on Sri Lanka's south and south-east coasts, including Kosgoda and Rakawa. They are, however, quite rare in Sri Lanka.
Olive Ridley Turtle This species has been observed nesting at Karaduwa, Maggona, Bundala, Marichchikaddi, Kalpitiya, Udappuwa, Hambantota, Kundakuliya, Chilaw, Negambo, Matara, and Weligama in Sri Lanka.
Leatherback Turtle Induruwa, Mawella, Ussangoda, Rekawa, Ambalangoda, Kosgoda, Bundala, Godavaya, and Yala are among the places where these turtles have been spotted nesting.
