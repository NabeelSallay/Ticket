Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery

The Hatchery

The Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery is a popular tourist attraction and conservation project located in Hikkaduwa, a coastal town in Sri Lanka. The hatchery is dedicated to protecting sea turtles and their eggs, as well as raising awareness about the importance of conservation.

The Goal

The primary goal of the hatchery is to provide a safe environment for turtle eggs to hatch and release the baby turtles into the sea. The hatchery collects eggs that are vulnerable to poaching or other threats and keeps them in protected areas until they are ready to hatch. Once the baby turtles emerge from their shells, they are released into the ocean.

Location

Hikkaduwa Turtle hatchery is located in the coastal town of Hikkaduwa, Sri Lanka. Hikkaduwa is located on the country's southwest coast, about 98 kilometers (61 miles) south of Colombo, the Sri Lankan capital. The hatchery is located on the main coastal road in Hikkaduwa, a popular tourist destination known for its beautiful beaches, coral reefs and marine life.
