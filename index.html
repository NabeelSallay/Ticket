    The Turtle Population in Sri Lanka

    Sri Lanka's turtle populations have faced serious threats over the years including habitat destruction, pollution, illegal fishing practices, and poaching for meat, eggs, and shellfish. These factors have led to declining turtle populations in many areas.However,Sri Lanka has taken several conservation measures to protect its turtle populations. Efforts include creating marine sanctuaries, enforcing tough anti-poaching and egg harvesting laws, raising awareness among local communities, and promoting sustainable fishing practices.

    Where To See Sea Turtles In Sri Lanka

    Watching turtles is an amazing experience that cannot be explained. Watching these calm and peaceful creatures swim slowly in the sea, looking for their next meal or retreating to a sandy beach to build a nest is sure to leave a lasting impression.Depending on the time of year, Rekawa, Hikkaduwa, Mirissa, Kosgoda, Beruwala and Trincomalee are considered the best spots on the island to see these magnificent turtles...or swim with them if you're lucky.

    Turtle Conservation In Sri Lanka

    Conservation organizations, government agencies and local communities are actively involved in conservation initiatives, including monitoring nest sites, protecting nests and releasing chicks into the sea. These efforts have had positive results in protecting and restoring turtle populations in some regions.

    Types of Turtles In Sri Lanka

    There are five different breeds of turtles in Sri Lanka which consist of: Olive Ridley Turtle (Lepidochelys olivacea),Green Turtle (Chelonia mydas),Loggerhead Turtle (Caretta caretta),Hawksbill Turtle (Eretmochelys imbricata),Leatherback Turtle (Dermochelys coriacea).
